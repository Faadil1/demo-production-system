# DPS Constitution

## I — Purpose

DPS exists to transform product understanding into a credible, reproducible, and explainable demonstration.

## II — Truth

Truth takes priority over aesthetics. Generated media may explain; it may not impersonate product evidence.

## III — Understanding

Rendering cannot begin until the system has produced and verified a sufficient understanding artifact.

## IV — Explainability

Every critical decision must record its question, considered options, chosen option, reason, confidence, and authoring agent or human.

## V — Reproducibility

A demonstration must be rebuildable from its versioned configuration, artifacts, decisions, assets, and adapter versions.

## VI — Replaceability

External providers are adapters. No provider may become part of the domain model.

## VII — Human Authority

The system may propose and automate, but humans retain final authority at declared approval gates.

## VIII — Evidence

A claim without observable support is a hypothesis. A demonstration must distinguish claims, hypotheses, and verified evidence.

## IX — Evolution

Engines, adapters, and models may evolve. Constitutional design laws change only through an explicit RFC.

## X — Learning

DPS learns from traceable decisions and measured outcomes, never from ungrounded aesthetic preference alone.
